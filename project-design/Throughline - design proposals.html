Design proposals — Apr 18 2026

Three screens to finish the throughline.

Composer for new goals and projects, a Threads view that treats each life-line as literal ink, and a Timeline that lays the year flat so you can see where you pivoted.

1Add a goal or project

A composer, not a form.

Declaring a new goal is a meaningful act — the UI should feel that way. One modal, one giant editorial prompt, a tab to switch between life goal and project. Projects always ask the single most important question: which goal does this feed? A "Standalone" pill is available for the things that don't fit — but it's off to the side so attachment stays the default.

Design notes
  • Tab toggle, not a radio — the two types share enough structure to feel like flavors of the same gesture.
  • The name input uses display type at 40px. You're writing a title, not filling a form.
  • "Feeds into" is a single horizontal pill row. If you have > 5 goals the row wraps; if you have 2 it stays tight. Scale without effort.
  • Project color inherits the parent goal's color by default — visual hierarchy follows attachment.
  • Secondary entry point: a dashed "+ new thread" tile at the bottom of each Sidebar section, so adding something new is always one click from where you'd look for it.

Entry points

From the sidebar, a dashed "+ New life goal" and "+ New project" tile sit at the bottom of each section. Click opens the composer above. From the Big Lines bar (when layout=top), an empty slot shows the same dashed style — clicking an empty slot opens the composer pre-selected to that slot.

Editing is the same composer, pre-filled. No separate edit screen.

2Threads view

Your life as ink on a line.

Each goal is a horizontal spine running across the screen. Captures are beads on the line, placed by date. Projects are sub-spines indented under their parent goal. Starred captures bloom into slightly larger beads in gold; pivots are vertical tick marks that break the line. At a glance you see rhythm and gaps — the months where one thread went quiet while another lit up.

Design notes
  • The spine IS the metaphor. Keeping each thread to a single horizontal line — no cards, no columns — makes the metaphor literal. The eye reads left-to-right as time, every time.
  • Bead size encodes importance: regular 7px, signal 11px with soft halo, pivot is a vertical bar that literally breaks the line.
  • A single "NOW" mark on every line anchors the eye. You see how stale or fresh each thread is relative to today.
  • Click any bead → opens the capture in a side panel. Click the thread label → drills into the Thread detail shown below.
  • Projects are indented and have a smaller bead size, so the parent goal always reads as the primary line.
3Timeline view

One line for the whole year.

The year laid out horizontally. Above the line, bars rising for each week's capture density — gold portions for signals. Crossing the line, vertical ticks for pivot moments, each with a short label. Above everything, ribbons showing when each goal and project was active. Click any week to expand its signals below. This is the "year in review" you can scroll through in 10 seconds.

Design notes
  • A single horizontal year line is the unifying element — pivots interrupt it, bars rise from it, ribbons run above it. Everything anchors to the same axis.
  • Bars show two stacked segments per week: total activity (ink) + signal portion (gold). The gold always grows from the base so you read density and quality together.
  • Future weeks are drawn as faint stubs so the year always feels whole — you're not looking at "blank space", you're looking at "what's to come."
  • Ribbons let you answer "what was I actually working on in April" without reading any text — patterns emerge visually.
  • Clicking a week expands it below with the signals rewritten as an editorial paragraph — the week becomes a tiny essay, not a list.
